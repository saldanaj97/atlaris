## Generation Lifecycle Consolidation

Primary commit: `8e6045cb` `refactor: consolidate generation lifecycle`

What shipped:

- Introduced `src/features/plans/session/plan-generation-session.ts` as the main create/retry streaming authority.
- Reduced `src/app/api/v1/plans/stream/route.ts` and `src/app/api/v1/plans/[planId]/retry/route.ts` to thinner HTTP adapters over feature-owned lifecycle/session code.
- Split stream responsibilities into focused modules for emitters, cleanup, DB lifetime, and terminal outcomes.
- Narrowed `PlanLifecycleService` behind a shared creation pipeline plus explicit AI/PDF origin strategies.
- Broke the AI orchestrator into smaller failure, reservation, provider-invocation, and timeout modules.
- Split job-query and attempt-persistence logic by responsibility instead of keeping those flows concentrated in large procedural files.

Why it mattered:

- Generation now has one real backend authority instead of route helpers, feature modules, and DB helpers each owning overlapping pieces of the same workflow.
- The lifecycle flow became easier to reason about, easier to test, and safer to extend in later client/API slices.

Notable file areas:

## Outcome

By the end of April 15, the branch had one coherent backend generation path for create and retry flows, with thinner routes and clearer ownership around orchestration, persistence, and stream side effects.

The core win was depth: lifecycle behavior moved out of shallow adapters and into modules that actually own the generation workflow.
